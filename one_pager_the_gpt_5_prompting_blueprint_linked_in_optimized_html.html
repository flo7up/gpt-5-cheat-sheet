Seven tactics for sharper answers, faster turnarounds, and fewer wasted reasoning tokens.

The GPT-5 Prompting Cheat Sheet

Works for any GPT‑5 app with a built-in model router incl. Microsoft Copilot and ChatGPT.
1

Think deeper / harder

Force multi-pass reasoning: generate alternatives, compare, then justify the choice.

Example: Analyze this marketing strategy from a CEO, customer, and competitor perspective; recommend the best approach. Think Harder
2

Ultra prompting

Explicitly invoke a longer reasoning mode: break down → clarify → plan → validate.

Example: Spend up to 5 minutes listing all plausible causes of declining sales, question each assumption, then rank the top three drivers.
Questions your own assumptions and take into account as many perspectives as possible. Evaluate the final result, identify gaps and compare alternative approaches.
3

Plan before answering

Require a structured plan (tasks, clarifications, approach, validation) before final output.

Decompose Clarify Approach Validate
Example: First draft a step-by-step plan for a renewable energy adoption whitepaper. Details each step. Set up evaluate criteria for the result. Then execute the plan.
4

Mental to‑do list

Have the model surface objective, subtasks, risks, and success checks up front.

Objective Subtasks Risks Criteria
Example: Produce: objective, 5 subtasks, 3 risks with mitigations, and acceptance criteria for the launch brief.
5

Structured prompting

Enter your prompt in (XML / JSON). Typically requires breaking down the problem into smaller parts or different areas already.

Example:
<brief>
  <goal>Improve onboarding conversion</goal>
  <audience>New SaaS users</audience>
  <constraints>Keep dev scope < 2 weeks</constraints>
  <metrics>ActivationRate,Day7Retention</metrics>
</brief>
Then ask: “Identify gaps and propose 3 refinements.”
6

Iterative refinement

Self‑evaluate against a numeric quality bar; revise until threshold met.

Draft Score Revise Finalize
Example: Draft the proposal, self‑score clarity 1–10; if <9 refine and list changes made.
7

Metaprompting

Ask the model how to improve the prompt without altering intent; apply safe adjustments.

Example: Suggest 3 prompt tweaks to increase specificity while preserving scope; explain each tweak’s benefit.
